    What it does

    Four jobs, done before your coffee.

    Flaily isn't another rule you have to maintain. It reads each message the way you would, decides what deserves your attention, and gets the boring part out of the way.

    Surfaces what matters

    Stars and labels the mail you can't afford to miss (invoices, payroll, government, tax, insurance, legal, deadlines and security alerts) even when no reply is needed.

    Drafts your replies

    When a message needs a personal answer, Flaily writes a ready-to-send draft and attaches the document you were asked for. It never sends; you just read, tweak and hit send.

    Blocks the noise

    Ask it to block a sender and their future mail skips your inbox for good. The one thing Flaily ever does to your inbox, and only when you say so.

    Tells you when it breaks

    If the Gmail connection ever expires, Flaily flags it loudly instead of failing in silence, so you're never left thinking it's working when it isn't.

    How it works

    Three steps. Then you forget it's there.

    1. 1

      Connect your Gmail

      A one-time, revocable connection that lets Flaily read your mail and prepare drafts, nothing else.

    2. 2

      It triages, twice a day

      On a schedule, Flaily scans your last 30 days of unread mail, stars and labels what matters, and drafts the replies that need one.

    3. 3

      You skim the summary

      A short report tells you exactly what it found and what it drafted. You approve the sends. That's the whole job.

    Privacy by design

    Your inbox stays yours.

    Flaily is built to be the most cautious assistant in your inbox. The defaults are conservative on purpose.

    • It never sends. Every reply is a draft waiting for your approval.
    • It never deletes. Triage means starring and labelling. Your mail is never thrown away.
    • One outbound action. Blocking a sender is the only thing it does to your inbox, and only when you ask.
    • Read only what it needs. It looks at unread mail from the last 30 days, not your whole archive.
    • Official rails. Built on Google's Gmail API and Anthropic's Claude. No scraping, no shady access.
    FAQ

    The questions everyone asks.

    Does it send emails on my behalf?

    No. Flaily only ever writes a draft. You read it, edit it if you like, and decide whether to send. Nothing leaves your account without you.

    Can it read my entire mailbox?

    It reads unread mail from the last 30 days so it can triage what's recent and still pending. Older and already-read mail is left alone.

    What happens with a sensitive email?

    It stars and labels it so you see it immediately. It doesn't act on it, forward it, or reply to it. Sensitive mail is surfaced, never handled.

    Can I try it right now?

    Flaily runs privately against its owner's Gmail as a scheduled agent, so there's no shared live demo to log into. This page walks through exactly how it behaves, and the code lives on GitHub.

    What is it built with?

    It's a Claude Code skill that runs on a schedule, using Anthropic's Claude to read and reason about each message and the Gmail API to star, label, draft and block.
